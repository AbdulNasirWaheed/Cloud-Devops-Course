## AWS Regions & Availability Zones

### What is a Region?

A **Region** is a geographic area where AWS has data centers.

**Examples:** Tokyo, Frankfurt, Sydney, Virginia, Mumbai

> Choose a region closest to your users for faster access.
>
> **Real-life example:** If you run a global online store, store Asian customer data in the Asia region and American customer data in the US region.

### What is an Availability Zone (AZ)?

An **Availability Zone** is like an individual data center inside a region.

- Each region has **2–6 AZs**
- If one AZ fails (power/hardware issue), the others keep running

> **Real-life analogy:** A shopping mall with several branches in the same city. If one branch closes for repairs, customers go to another nearby branch.

### Why Do Regions & AZs Matter?

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| High Availability | App stays up if one zone has issues |
| Data Residency | Keep data within a specific country |
| Performance | Serve users from the closest region |

**Summary:**
- **Region** = geographic area (country or group of countries)
- **AZ** = individual data center inside the region
- Together → AWS is reliable, fast, and globally accessible

## How to Create a Free AWS Account

### Step 1 — Go to AWS Website
1. Open: [https://aws.amazon.com](https://aws.amazon.com)
2. Click **"Create a Free Account"**

### Step 2 — Enter Account Information
1. Enter a valid email address
2. Choose an AWS account name (e.g., your name or business name)
3. Click **"Verify email address"**
4. Enter the OTP sent to your email

### Step 3 — Set Root User Password
1. Create a strong password and confirm it
2. Click **"Continue"**

### Step 4 — Choose Account Type
1. Select **"Personal"** (for learning) or **"Professional"** (for business)
2. Fill in: Full name, Phone number, Country, Address, City, Postal code
3. Agree to the AWS Customer Agreement
4. Click **"Continue"**

### Step 5 — Add Payment Information
1. Enter credit/debit card details (Visa or MasterCard recommended)
2. AWS charges a small **$1 verification hold** (refunded later)
3. Verify with OTP from your bank if prompted

> 💡 AWS Free Tier gives you **12 months free**, but a card is still required.

### Step 6 — Identity Verification
1. Enter your mobile number
2. Choose verification method: **SMS** or **Voice call**
3. Enter the PIN received to verify your phone

### Step 7 — Select a Support Plan
1. Choose **"Basic Plan" (Free)**
2. Click **"Complete Sign Up"**

### ✅ Done! Next Steps
1. Log in to the AWS Console: [https://console.aws.amazon.com](https://console.aws.amazon.com)
2. Sign in with your email + password as the **root user**
